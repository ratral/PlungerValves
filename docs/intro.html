Chapter 1 Introduction

Plunger valves are mainly used where volume flows or pressure have to be reduced and regulated with out vibrations, noises or big changes in the pipeline.

1.2 Flow variable and fluid properties

The flow rate of a fluid through a control valve is a function of the following (where applicable):

  • Inlet and outlet conditions
    • Pressure
    • Temperature
    • Piping geometry
  • Water properties
    • Composition
    • Density
    • Vapor pressure
    • Viscosity
    • Surface tension
    • Thermodynamic critical pressure
  • Control valve properties
    • Size
    • Valve travel
    • Flow path geometry

1.4 Terms and definitions

  • Valve: A valve is a device used for the control of fluid flow. It consists of a fluid retaining assembly, one or more ports between end openings and a movable closure member which opens, restricts or closes the port(s).

  • Control valve: A operated (manual or powered) device which modifies the fluid flow rate in a process control system. It consists of a valve connected to an actuator mechanism that is capable of changing the position of a flow controlling element in the valve in response to a signal from the controlling system.
